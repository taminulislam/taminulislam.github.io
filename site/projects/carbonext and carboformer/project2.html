CarboNeXT and CarboFormer: Dual Semantic Segmentation Architectures for Detecting and Quantifying Carbon Dioxide Emissions Using Optical Gas Imaging

Taminul Islam, Toqi Tahamid Sarker, Mohamed G. Embaby, Khaled R. Ahmed, Amer AbuGhazaleh
Southern Illinois University, Carbondale, IL, USA
arXiv Preprint Paper Code Datasets

Carbon dioxide (CO‚ÇÇ) emissions are critical indicators of both environmental impact and various industrial processes, including livestock management. We introduce CarboNeXT, a semantic segmentation framework for Optical Gas Imaging (OGI), designed to detect and quantify CO‚ÇÇ emissions across diverse applications, and CarboFormer, a lightweight variant optimized for resource-constrained platforms such as programmable drones.

üéØ CarboNeXT Architecture

CarboNeXT Framework: Multi-scale transformer-based encoder-decoder structure with UPerHead and auxiliary FCN components. The architecture effectively models both local details and global relationships in gas plume imagery through sophisticated attention mechanisms and hierarchical feature extraction, achieving state-of-the-art performance in CO‚ÇÇ plume segmentation.

üöÅ CarboFormer Architecture

CarboFormer Lightweight Design: Optimized architecture with efficient attention mechanisms and model compression for resource-constrained platforms. With only 5.07M parameters, CarboFormer maintains competitive accuracy while enabling deployment on programmable drones and edge devices for real-time CO‚ÇÇ emission monitoring in the field.

üìä Datasets & Experimental Setup

üî¨ Controlled Carbon Dioxide Release (CCR) Dataset

CCR Dataset: Systematic CO‚ÇÇ emissions captured using FLIR G343 OGI camera under controlled conditions with systematically varied flow rates (10-100 SCCM). The dataset provides comprehensive coverage of different emission intensities, enabling robust model training and evaluation across various gas leak scenarios representative of industrial applications.

üêÑ Real Time ANKOM (RTA) Dataset

RTA Dataset: Real-time CO‚ÇÇ emissions from dairy cow rumen fluid in vitro experiments using ANKOM gas production module. This unique dataset captures the complex dynamics of biological CO‚ÇÇ generation, providing valuable insights for precision livestock management and environmental monitoring applications in agricultural settings.

üìä Experimental Results & Analysis

üèÜ CCR Dataset Performance

CCR Dataset Results: CarboNeXT achieves outstanding 88.46% mIoU on the controlled emission dataset, significantly outperforming state-of-the-art methods including DeepLabV3+, PSPNet, and U-Net variants. The results demonstrate superior performance across varied flow rates, with particular effectiveness in challenging low-flow scenarios critical for environmental monitoring applications.

üêÑ RTA Dataset Performance

RTA Dataset Results: CarboNeXT demonstrates exceptional performance with 92.95% mIoU on livestock emission data, while CarboFormer achieves competitive 92.98% mIoU with significantly fewer parameters. Both models excel in detecting biological CO‚ÇÇ emissions, validating their effectiveness for precision agriculture applications.

‚öñÔ∏è Performance vs Efficiency Trade-off

Comparative Analysis: Comprehensive evaluation showing CarboNeXT's superior accuracy versus CarboFormer's computational efficiency. CarboNeXT prioritizes detection precision for high-stakes applications, while CarboFormer balances performance with resource constraints, enabling deployment on drones and edge devices for field monitoring.

üìà Multi-Scale Flow Rate Analysis

Multi-Scale Detection Capability: Both architectures demonstrate robust performance across varied flow rates from 10-100 SCCM, with particularly strong performance in low-flow scenarios critical for environmental and livestock monitoring. The analysis validates the models' ability to detect subtle CO‚ÇÇ emissions that traditional methods might miss.
